Important developments by top-tier enterprises

Waymo Via, the autonomous delivery and logistics company owned by Google Alphabet, is testing L-4 autonomous trucks in Texas, Arizona, and California, leveraging their advanced sensor suite to handle most driving scenarios independently.

TuSimple, a trucking technology company that develops self-driving technology for long-haul freight transportation, also operates L-4 autonomous trucks on long-haul routes in the U.S. and China, focusing on reducing costs and improving efficiency in freight transportation.

Additionally, Gatik, a Silicon Valley based company in the autonomous technology industry, has deployed L-4 autonomous trucks for middle-mile logistics, running fully driverless routes for major retailers like Walmart in Arkansas. These examples highlight the current state of high automation in the autonomous trucking industry, where significant advancements are being made while still ensuring safety through human oversight when necessary.
